How to Evaluate a Food Packaging Supplier: A Buyer's Checklist

Selecting a food packaging supplier is one of the highest-leverage procurement decisions a food service operation makes. A poor choice costs far more than the unit price differential — it creates supply disruptions, quality incidents, and unplanned expense.

1. Food Safety Certifications

Any supplier providing food-contact packaging must demonstrate FDA compliance. Ask for FDA 21 CFR certification for all food-contact resins, SQF or BRC food safety management certification, HACCP plans, and migration testing documentation for rPET materials.

2. Lead Times and Inventory Practices

Standard lead times for stock packaging should be 3—10 business days. Ask how suppliers manage safety stock for high-volume SKUs and whether they offer blanket-order programs with scheduled releases — critical for seasonal demand variance.

3. Quality Control Systems

Mature QC includes statistical process control data, defined AQL sampling plans, dimensional tolerance documentation, and corrective action procedures for non-conforming product. Request all of these at evaluation.

4. Minimum Order Quantities

Evaluate MOQs at three levels: individual SKU minimums, mixed-SKU pallet programs, and custom tooling commitments. A supplier with high individual SKU MOQs but flexible mixed-pallet programs may still be workable depending on your inventory model.

5. Sustainability Documentation

If you have ESG reporting requirements, your supplier needs verifiable documentation: third-party certified recycled content percentages by SKU, recyclability designations, and compatibility with local programs. Marketing claims are not sufficient for buyer reporting.

6. Account Service Structure

Ask about dedicated account management, technical support access, and escalation procedures for quality incidents. A domestically-based supplier with a reachable team provides significantly more value when production issues arise compared to an overseas broker.
